# Editor-in-Chief Review Report

**Reviewer:** Editor-in-Chief, Symmetry (MDPI)
**Paper:** Symmetric versus Asymmetric Knowledge Distillation: A Comparative Study for Image Classification

---

## Journal Fit: 7/10

The paper's central framing—symmetry vs. asymmetry in knowledge transfer—is directly within Symmetry's scope. However, the paper engages with "symmetry" primarily as a descriptive label rather than as a mathematical concept (invariance, equivariance, group actions). For this journal, a deeper theoretical connection would substantially strengthen the manuscript.

## Originality: 6/10

The symmetric/asymmetric taxonomy for KD methods is a novel organizational lens. However, the paper's contributions are primarily taxonomic and empirical rather than theoretical. The connection to existing "instance-level vs. structure-level" distinctions is acknowledged but not fully differentiated.

## Significance: 6/10

Provides a useful framework for practitioners selecting KD methods. The empirical comparison under controlled conditions is valuable. However, the single-dataset, single-run methodology limits the generalizability and statistical robustness of the conclusions.

## Recommendation: Major Revision

### Required Improvements
1. Deepen the theoretical connection to symmetry (equivariance/invariance properties)
2. Acknowledge and differentiate from existing taxonomies more clearly
3. Add discussion of how the taxonomy could guide new method design
4. Consider renaming to avoid overclaiming (e.g., "A Symmetry-Based Perspective" rather than "A Comparative Study")
